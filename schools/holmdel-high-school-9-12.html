PRINCIPAL'S MESSAGE

October 15, 2019

Dear HHS Community,

Hope all is well following the long weekend!  Our incredible staff had the opportunity to take part in some great learning experiences during our Professional Development Day!  Please check out below for a couple of friendly reminders...

  • PSAT Testing Wednesday, October 16th---As noted in the Daily Announcements, our 10th and 11th graders will be taking the PSAT and should arrive to school at our regularly scheduled time.  Our 9th and 12th graders will not be taking the PSAT so they may arrive at 10:00.  Of course, if your child's only mode of transportation is the school bus they are welcome to join us at our regularly scheduled time in the morning. 
  • Breast Cancer Awareness Month---As you know, HHS always celebrates our Fridays with HHS/Holmdel attire or Holmdel blue and white.  Many groups here at HHS are running fundraisers and finding other ways to get the word our about Breast Cancer.  With that said, as another awareness opportunity for HHS, we welcome students/staff to continue with Holmdel blue and white or join us with pink attire all Fridays in the month of October.
  • Music Honor Society Induction Wednesday, October 16th---Always proud to showcase our students' successes in all areas at HHS!  Please feel free to join us tomorrow evening at 6:30 to celebrate many of our Music students!

As always, thanks for partnering with us to do great things for the HHS Community!

Brian Schillaci

 

 

September 23, 2019

Dear HHS Community,

Unbelievable to think we have just started our 4th week of the 2019-20 school year!  Thank you to students, staff, and families for helping to make this a very smooth start.  The students have done a great job following through with all the enhancements we put into place to begin the year.  Thank you for the support!

As many of you know, there are a great many student drop-offs in the morning...upwards of 275 cars on certain days.  In addition to all of our student and staff drivers, that brings a lot of activity to our parking lot.  With that said, if you are dropping off in the morning, please note drop-offs must take place only in designated areas...drop off loop at the main entrance, IN the Satz parking lot, or down the lane heading toward the Satz main entrance (beginning at approximately 7:10).  Parents dropping off should not be heading down the aisles in the main parking lot.  As you can imagine, this creates a very dangerous situation for our students and staff walking through the parking lot to the building at this very busy time.  Officer Martin and HHS administration will be in the parking lot area during this week to assist with any questions you may have about drop-off procedures.

As always, thanks for partnering with us to do great things for our HHS community!

Brian Schillaci
